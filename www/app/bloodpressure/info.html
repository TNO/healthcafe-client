Information about blood pressure

Blood pressure is the force with which the heart pumps blood through your veins. This creates a pressure on the vessels. With an elevated blood pressure (hypertension), this pressure is so high that the walls of the vessels get damaged.

Blood pressure consists of two values. The systolic blood pressure is the pressure that occurs when the heart contracts. The diastolic blood pressure is the pressure when the heart relaxes.

A healthy blood pressure has a systole of 135 mmHg or lower, and a diastole of 90 mmHg or lower.
