Information about cholesterol

Cholesterol is a fatlike substance that is essential for the production of body cells and hormones. There are different types of cholesterol.

A total cholesterol of less then 5,0 mmol/l is considered healthy. For LDL or the ‘bad cholesterol’ the healthy upper level is 2,5 mmol/l. For HDL or the ‘good cholesterol’ your values should be 0,9 mmol/l or higher.

Besides cholesterol, there is another fatty substance in your blood: triglycerides. This has an upper limit of 2,1 mmol/l.

Is your cholesterol too high?

The Dutch Nutrition council recommends to eat least 15 grams of unsalted nuts per day, based on scientific research.
