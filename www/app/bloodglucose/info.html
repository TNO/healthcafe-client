Information about blood glucose

If your blood glucose is above the threshold value you might appreciate this advices and tips below

Glucose is a form of sugar that is converted into energy by our body. Carbohydrates from food or drinks are absorbed into our blood after consumption. Our body works to keep the level of glucose in blood in balance. With diabetics, this mechanism is out of balance.

Did you measure your blood glucose in a fasting state (after at least 8 hours of fasting)? Then your blood glucose should be below 6,1 mmol/l.

Did you measure your blood glucose after a meal or a ‘challenge test’? Then your blood glucose should be below 7,8 mmol/l two hours after the meal.

Blood glucose too high?

The Dutch Nutrition Council recommends to minimize consumption of sugary beverages.

Try instead to drink green tea; drinking three to six cups a day, can help lower your blood glucose level More information can be foundhere.

Replace refined grain products by wholegrain product. For instance, eat at least 90 grams of brown bread, wholegrain bread or other wholegrain products. More information can be found here.
